WaveNODE: A Continuous Normalizing Flow for Speech Synthesis

Anonymous Authors
[Code]

Abstract

In recent years, various flow-based generative models have been proposed to generate high-fidelity waveforms in real-time. However, these models usually require either a well-trained teacher network or a number of flow steps making them memory-inefficient. In this paper, we propose a novel generative model called WaveNODE which leverages continuous normalizing flows for speech synthesis. Unlike the existing models, WaveNODE places no constraint on the type of the functions used in flow transformations. Moreover, WaveNODE can be optimized to maximize the likelihood without requiring any teacher network or auxiliary loss terms. We experimentally show that WaveNODE achieves comparable performance in terms of mean opinion score (MOS) and conditional log-likelihood (CLL) with much fewer parameters compared to the conventional models.
